What is DevOps

DevOps is an increasingly common approach to software distribution where development teams and operations teams work together to create, test, distribute and monitor applications with speed, quality and control.

The approach

The DevOps approach is relevant to any type of software project, regardless of architecture, platform or purpose.

Use cases

Common usage cases include: native cloud and mobile applications, application integration and multicloud modernization and management.

The implementations

Successful implementations of DevOps generally rely on an integrated set of solutions, or "toolchain", to remove manual operations, reduce errors and achieve greater size than small, isolated teams.

Why choose DevOps

Your best results, faster

Programmatic scalability and cost optimization

The scalability of an infrastructure is essential to ensure the expected business results. For this reason it is important that the software is able to govern the infrastructure easily and with maximum automation.

Results in less time

Continuous integration and continuous delivery practices allow you to fix bugs faster, integrate new features and collect feedback from users faster. In a competitive market, speed and time to market are key parameters.

Adapt to change

The agile methodology promotes the ability to "respond to change rather than follow a plan", using processes that are able to adapt with speed, released frequently to apply metrics capable of measuring the effectiveness of actions.
